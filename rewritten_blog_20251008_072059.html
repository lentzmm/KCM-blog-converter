South Jersey Home Prices: The Real Story Behind the Headlines




If you've been following real estate news lately, you've probably seen headlines claiming home prices are flat across the country. But here in South Jersey, the real story is much more nuanced. Whether you're looking in Haddonfield, Cherry Hill, Washington Township, Mullica Hill, or Pitman, the reality is that our local markets don't always follow national trends.

In most South Jersey towns, prices aren't flat at all – they're telling a much more interesting story.

What South Jersey's Housing Data Actually Shows

While we've certainly seen price moderation compared to the rapid climb of 2020-2022, South Jersey's five-county region (Gloucester, Camden, Burlington, Salem, and Cumberland) is experiencing varied price trends that largely depend on which town you're looking at and available inventory.

In fact, if we look at recent data across our region, we see Gloucester County towns like Woolwich and Mullica Hill still experiencing modest price increases due to their proximity to Philadelphia (just 25 minutes away) and limited inventory. Meanwhile, parts of Camden County such as Cherry Hill have seen prices stabilize, and some Cumberland County areas like Vineland have experienced slight cooling as more inventory has become available.

The big takeaway for South Jersey homeowners and buyers is that "flat" prices simply don't tell the complete story of what's happening on the ground in towns like Mount Laurel, Voorhees, Pitman, or Deptford. Price trends vary significantly depending on which side of Route 55 or I-295 you're looking.

One driving factor in our local market? Available inventory. As the Joint Center for Housing Studies (JCHS) of Harvard University explains:

" . . . price trends are beginning to diverge in markets across the country. Prices are declining in a growing number of markets where inventories have soared while they continue to climb in markets where for-sale inventories remain tight."

This perfectly describes what we're seeing across South Jersey. In Moorestown and Haddonfield, where inventory remains extremely limited, prices continue to inch upward. Meanwhile, in areas of Gloucester Township and parts of Salem County where more homes have come on the market, we're seeing modest price adjustments.

Even in areas experiencing slight corrections, most South Jersey homeowners remain well ahead on their investments. Properties in towns like Sewell, Marlton, and Medford have seen roughly 45-50% appreciation over the past five years, putting most homeowners in a strong equity position despite any recent moderation.

If You're Selling in South Jersey...

You need to understand how your specific town's market is performing, as pricing strategies vary significantly across our five counties.

  • In towns like Moorestown, Cherry Hill, and Mullica Hill where inventory remains tight, you may still enjoy a relatively straightforward selling experience with fewer concessions. A recent 3-bedroom ranch in Mount Laurel sold within four days with no inspection contingencies.
  • In areas like parts of Woodbury, Williamstown, or Vineland where more inventory is available, precise pricing becomes critical. We recently advised a seller in Deptford to price their home $15,000 lower than they initially wanted, resulting in multiple offers and a final price $10,000 above their original target.

South Jersey sellers also need to understand our unique New Jersey closing process, including the 3-day attorney review period and the seller's responsibility for realty transfer taxes (approximately $2-$4 per $500 of purchase price, with additional fees for homes over $1 million). Proper preparation for these New Jersey-specific requirements can make your transaction much smoother.
